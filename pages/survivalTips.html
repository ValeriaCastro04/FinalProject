📚 Study Smarter, Not Harder

  • 🧠 Understand > Memorize — ESEN loves analysis and application. Know the “why” behind everything.
  • 📅 Create a weekly study plan — Block time for every subject (and breaks too!). Google Calendar is your bestie.
  • 👥 Form or join a study group — Don’t wait until finals. Two minds are better than one. Five is a power move.
  • 📱 Use tech tools — Notion, Quizlet, Anki, and ChatGPT can save hours of your life.
  • 🪫 Know your limits — If your brain is fried, REST. No badge of honor for burnout.

😵‍💫 Avoiding Meltdown 101

  • 😴 Sleep is sacred — Pulling all-nighters messes up your focus and mood. Try 7 hours, minimum.
  • 💧 Hydrate, stretch, move — Water and short walks actually boost retention and energy.
  • 📣 Talk to someone — Professors, friends, or counselors. Don’t keep it all in your head.
  • 🎯 Focus on progress, not perfection — 80% done is better than 0% perfect.

💼 Business School = Leadership Playground

You'll take classes like Leadership Fundamentals, Total Wellness, and Teamwork — but don’t treat them like "extra" subjects. They're tools for life and for every career ahead.


  • 👔 Step up during group work — This is where real leadership starts (not just in theory).
  • 🙋 Ask questions and participate — These subjects reward curiosity and authenticity.
  • 🧘 Take the wellness part seriously — You’ll be surprised how much it affects your productivity.

🔥 Power Tips from Survivors

  • 📎 Keep all your files in Google Drive — organized by course and week. Your future self will thank you.
  • 🎧 Study playlists are magic — Lo-fi, jazz, movie soundtracks… whatever gets you in the zone.
  • 🙌 Celebrate the small wins — Turned in an assignment? SURVIVED AN EXAM? That’s huge.
  • 📚 Don’t be afraid to visit professors during office hours — most love helping students who show interest.
  • 🧾 Learn to love the syllabus — It’s basically a roadmap to survive each class.

💬 Final Thought

You’re not alone in this — everyone at ESEN has had a “what am I doing here?” moment. What matters most is that you ask for help when needed, stay open to feedback, and keep going. 🌱


You’re not just here to pass courses — you’re here to become the kind of leader the world actually needs. No pressure 😅
